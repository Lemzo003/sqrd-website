Temporary Works Engineering

Practical Temporary Works design and checks for contractors, TWCs and site teams.

SQRD focuses exclusively on Temporary Works. Designs and checks are delivered in line with BS 5975, relevant Eurocodes and supplier guidance — with buildability and site practicality in mind.

BS 5975 • Eurocodes • Supplier systems • Contractor-focused

Delivery & approach

Every enquiry is reviewed individually. Programme and fee depend on:

  • Temporary Works category
  • Complexity & risk level
  • Information provided
  • Required timescale / urgency

A written confirmation email is issued before work starts, summarising scope, assumptions, fee and indicative delivery.

Temporary Works Services

Ready-to-use scope options you can reference directly in your TW brief.

  • Formwork and falsework design for slabs, walls, beams and rafts
  • Backpropping and re-shoring layouts and design checks
  • Temporary propping of RC walls, cores and structural frames
  • Retaining systems, kingpost walls and excavation support checks
  • Working platforms and access platforms (including MEWP access)
  • Scaffold platforms and birdcages (TG20 / supplier supported)
  • Hoist landing platforms and tie arrangements
  • Edge protection to steel and concrete structures
  • Lifting beams, spreader frames and A-frames
  • Precast lifting and handling verification
  • Crane mats and working platform assessments
  • Local RC slab or foundation checks for concentrated loads
  • Steel member checks and local strengthening details

Simply reference these items when emailing (e.g. “TW Request – Working Platform – Item 5”).

Process

A simple approach aligned with BS 5975 Temporary Works procedures.

  • 1. Send TW brief — drawings, loads, standards, category and programme.
  • 2. Written proposal — scope, assumptions, agreement and indicative delivery.
  • 3. Design / check completed — calculations, sketches and notes prepared.
  • 4. Issue for your TW procedure — for TWC / Cat 2 / Cat 3 internal review.
