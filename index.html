Temporary Works Engineering

Temporary Works design & checks for live construction projects.

Dedicated to Temporary Works only. Designs and checks in line with BS 5975, relevant Eurocodes and supplier guidance, with buildability and site constraints at the centre.

SQRD supports contractors, TWCs and site teams with clear, buildable solutions for formwork, platforms, propping, retaining systems, lifting frames and more.

BS 5975 • Eurocodes • Supplier systems • Contractor-focused engineering

Delivery & approach

Each enquiry is scoped individually. Programme and fee depend on:

  • Temporary Works category (BS 5975)
  • Complexity and risk level
  • Information provided at the start
  • Required timescale / urgency

A short written proposal is issued before work starts, confirming scope, assumptions, fee and indicative delivery. If the brief changes, the proposal is updated by agreement.

Temporary Works services

A clear set of services you can reference directly in your email brief (for example: “TW Request – Working platform – item 5”).

  • Formwork and falsework design for slabs, walls, beams and rafts
  • Backpropping and re-shoring layouts and checks
  • Temporary propping of RC walls, cores and frames
  • Retaining systems, kingpost walls and excavation support checks
  • Working platforms and access platforms (including MEWP access)
  • Scaffold platforms and birdcages (TG20 / supplier-based)
  • Hoist landing platforms and tie arrangements
  • Edge protection to steel and concrete structures
  • Lifting beams, spreader frames and A-frames
  • Precast lifting and handling verification
  • Crane mats and working platform assessments
  • Local RC slab or foundation checks for concentrated loads
  • Steel member checks and local strengthening details

You remain in control of TW categories, internal checks and permits. SQRD supports the design, checking and documentation.

How it works

A simple process aligned with BS 5975 Temporary Works procedures.

  • 1. Send TW brief – drawings, loads, standards, TW category (if known) and programme.
  • 2. Written proposal – scope, assumptions, fee and indicative delivery are emailed to you.
  • 3. Design / check completed – calculations, sketches and notes are prepared.
  • 4. Issue for your TW procedure – for TWC / Cat 2 / Cat 3 review and site implementation.
Standards & tools
  • BS 5975 Temporary Works procedures
  • Relevant Eurocodes and National Annexes
  • Supplier technical data (scaffold, formwork, anchors, systems)
  • Autodesk Robot Structural Analysis + hand calculations & spreadsheets
